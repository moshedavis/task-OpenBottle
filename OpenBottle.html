The art and science of wine-bottle opening - method 1

It can be a real bummer to relax in anticipation of filling your wine glass, and to realize that you don't have a corkscrew. Here's a method to remove the cork even without a corkscrew.

Prerequisites

  • A corked bottle of wine (not a screw cap)
  • An impact-absorbing material (for instance, a thick towel or a shoe)

The procedure

  1. Remove any foil covering from the cork.
  2. Wrap the heel (bottom) of the bottle with the shock-absorbing material (for a shoe, place the heel of the bottle in the heel of the shoe).
  3. With medium force, strike the heel of the bottle repeatedly against a solid structure (such as a wall or tree).
  4. After about a dozen thumps, you will see the cork slowly popping out of the bottle.
  5. Repeat until the cork has popped out of the bottle enough to grab it firmly.
  6. Finish pulling the cork out by hand.

Conclusion

After all of your effort, relax and enjoy!
